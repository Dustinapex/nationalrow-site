About the I-69 Connector Project

The I-69 Connector is a major TxDOT initiative to extend Interstate 69 into the Rio Grande Valley, connecting the region to the national highway system through Cameron, Hidalgo, and Willacy counties. The project requires acquisition of right-of-way from private landowners along the new corridor.

What TxDOT's Offer May Miss

Interstate-grade corridor acquisitions in South Texas involve some of the most complex ROW valuation issues in the state. TxDOT's initial offer may significantly undercount your damages.

Agricultural Land Value

South Texas farmland has specialized agricultural value — citrus, vegetables, sorghum, livestock — that generic appraisals may undervalue.

Water Rights & Irrigation

Water rights and irrigation infrastructure are critical assets in the Valley. Taking land that includes irrigation ditches, canals, or wells demands careful valuation.

Severance of Farm Operations

When a new interstate divides a working farm, the separated tracts may lose viability for farming operations — a significant and compensable damage.

Remainder Tract Value Loss

Land remaining after the acquisition may have diminished value due to shape, size, access restrictions, or proximity to the new road.

Mineral Rights Considerations

Where subsurface mineral rights are involved, the impact of a surface taking on those rights may have value implications not captured in TxDOT's offer.

Relocation & Displacement

If your home or business is in the take area, federal relocation assistance programs may provide additional benefits beyond the land acquisition price.

Frequently Asked Questions — I-69 Connector

The I-69 Connector is a federal interstate — does that change my rights?

Federal interstate projects in Texas are still administered by TxDOT and subject to Texas eminent domain law. You have the same rights to negotiate, present an independent appraisal, and contest the amount of compensation.

Which counties are affected by the I-69 Connector?

The I-69 Connector project affects Cameron, Hidalgo, and Willacy counties in South Texas. If your property is in any of these counties and you've been contacted by TxDOT, call us.

Is National ROW a law firm?

No. National ROW is a right-of-way consulting firm, not a law firm. We do not provide legal advice. We help landowners understand their options, quantify damages, and negotiate with TxDOT.

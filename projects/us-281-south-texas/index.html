What Their Offer May Miss

The agency's appraisers work for the agency — not for you. Their initial offer routinely omits significant categories of compensation you may be entitled to under Texas law.

Ranching & Agricultural Value

South Texas ranching operations, livestock grazing, and dryland farming have active income value that standard appraisals may discount or ignore entirely.

Remainder Tract Damage

When TxDOT takes a strip from your ranch or farm, the remaining land may lose usability, access, or market value — all compensable under Texas eminent domain law.

Fence & Infrastructure Replacement

Miles of ranch fencing, cattle guards, water wells, and stock tanks are real assets that must be accurately valued and fully replaced or compensated.

Access & Operational Disruption

Loss of direct highway access, cattle crossing points, or equipment access routes can severely impact ranch operations and permanently reduce land value.

Mineral & Surface Rights

In South Texas oil and gas country, the interaction between surface takings and subsurface mineral rights deserves careful independent analysis and valuation.

Severance of Ranch Tracts

Highway expansion that splits a working ranch into non-contiguous pieces may eliminate operational viability — a significant and fully compensable severance loss.

Frequently Asked Questions

TxDOT said their offer is fair — is that true?

TxDOT is required to make a good-faith offer, but "good faith" doesn't mean maximum value. Their appraisers work for TxDOT, not for you. An independent review often reveals additional compensable items that TxDOT's appraiser didn't include.

Can I negotiate if I haven't signed yet?

Yes — and that's the best time to negotiate. Before you sign anything, you have maximum leverage. Get an independent review immediately before responding to TxDOT.

Is National ROW a law firm?

No. National ROW is a right-of-way consulting firm, not a law firm. We do not provide legal advice. We help landowners understand their options, quantify damages, and negotiate compensation.

What if my property is a working ranch — does that change my case?

Yes, in a good way for you. Working ranches have operational value, income value, livestock infrastructure, and potential severance damages that go well beyond simple per-acre land value. These cases often have significant additional compensation available.

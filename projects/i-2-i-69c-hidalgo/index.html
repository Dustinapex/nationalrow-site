About the I-2 / I-69C Interchange Project

The I-2/I-69C interchange reconstruction in Hidalgo County is one of the most significant infrastructure improvements in the Rio Grande Valley. TxDOT's Pharr District is acquiring right-of-way from private landowners to accommodate the expanded interchange footprint and associated improvements.

What Their Offer May Miss

The agency's appraisers work for the agency — not for you. Their initial offer routinely omits significant categories of compensation you may be entitled to under Texas law.

Frontage Road Access Loss

Properties losing direct frontage road access due to interchange reconstruction may suffer significant access damages TxDOT doesn't proactively account for.

Commercial Visibility & Exposure

Businesses near interchange areas rely on visibility and traffic flow. Changes to ramps and access points directly affect commercial property values.

Remainder Tract Damage

Partial takings at interchanges often leave oddly shaped or landlocked remainder parcels with reduced utility and market value.

Agricultural Severance

Farm and ranchland cut by interchange expansions may lose operational viability for the severed tract, creating compensable severance damage.

Utility Relocation

Water, sewer, gas, and electrical infrastructure within the take area may need to be relocated — costs that must be properly accounted for in your offer.

Improvements & Structures

Buildings, fences, irrigation infrastructure, and other improvements within the take area must be accurately valued, not just estimated.

Frequently Asked Questions

I own property near the I-2/I-69C interchange — am I affected?

If TxDOT has contacted you or sent a written offer, you are in the acquisition process. Even if you haven't been contacted yet, properties near the interchange footprint may be acquired. Contact us early for the best outcome.

Can I get more than what TxDOT offered?

In most cases, yes. TxDOT's initial offer is based on their appraiser's opinion of value. An independent appraisal frequently identifies additional compensable items. Our clients regularly receive more than the initial offer.

Is National ROW a law firm?

No. National ROW is a right-of-way consulting firm, not a law firm. We do not provide legal advice. We help landowners understand their options, quantify damages, and negotiate compensation.

What Is the ERCOT 765kV Transmission Project?

ERCOT's Long-Range Transmission Plan (LRTP) calls for a new 765-kilovolt high-voltage backbone across Texas — the largest grid expansion in state history. Transmission companies like Oncor, AEP Texas, and others are now acquiring right-of-way from private landowners to build the lines.

What ERCOT's Offer May Miss

Transmission companies are required to compensate you — but their initial offer often leaves out significant categories of damages that you may be entitled to under Texas law.

Diminution in Land Value

A high-voltage transmission easement can permanently reduce the market value of your entire tract, not just the strip acquired.

Crop & Agricultural Losses

Construction and permanent easement restrictions can eliminate crop income during and after construction.

Severance Damages

If the easement divides your property, the separated portions may lose value — compensation you're entitled to claim.

Timber & Vegetation Removal

Trees, brush, and vegetation cleared for the easement corridor have real value that must be quantified and compensated.

Access & Operational Restrictions

Future building restrictions, equipment access routes, and use limitations under the easement have lasting economic impact.

Proximity to Structures

The distance of towers and lines from your home, barn, or other improvements affects property marketability and value.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have to accept ERCOT's (or the transmission company's) offer?

No. You have the right to negotiate. While the company can ultimately pursue condemnation through eminent domain, you are entitled to just compensation — and the initial offer is rarely the final word.

What is a 765kV easement and how does it affect my land?

A 765kV transmission easement is a permanent right granted to the utility to construct, operate, and maintain high-voltage lines on a strip of your land. It limits how you can use that strip and may affect the value of your entire property.

Is National ROW a law firm?

No. National ROW is a right-of-way consulting firm, not a law firm. We do not provide legal advice. We help landowners understand their options, quantify damages, and negotiate compensation. We work alongside attorneys when legal representation is needed.

How much does it cost to hire National ROW?

Nothing upfront. We work on a contingency basis — our fee is a percentage of any increase we secure above the original offer. If we don't improve your offer, you owe us nothing.

How quickly do I need to act?

Once you sign an easement agreement, it is permanent and very difficult to modify. If you've received an offer or a representative has contacted you, you should seek an independent review before signing.

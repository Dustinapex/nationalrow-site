About the Toby Keith Expressway Project

The Toby Keith Expressway — officially OTA's East-West Connector — is a new 28-mile turnpike connecting I-44 near the Tri-City area (Newcastle, Blanchard, and Tuttle) to I-35 in the Moore/Norman area, then continuing east to I-40 near the Kickapoo Turnpike. Part of the ACCESS Oklahoma long-range plan, the project is advancing through active design on multiple segments, with right-of-way acquisition already underway on the first segment between I-44 and I-35 — roughly 34 properties needed, about 14 already bought, and around 20 still open, with acquisition expected to run through the end of 2026.

What Their Offer May Miss

The agency's appraisers work for the agency — not for you. Their initial offer routinely omits significant categories of compensation you may be entitled to under Oklahoma law.

Rapidly Appreciating Land Value

Land along the new I-44-to-I-35 corridor near Newcastle, Moore, and Norman has appreciated quickly. OTA's appraisal date may lag actual current market conditions.

Development Potential Loss

Land near new interchanges along the Toby Keith Expressway may carry significant future development potential. If a taking eliminates your ability to develop that land, the lost potential is fully compensable.

Remainder Tract Damage

A new turnpike alignment can leave landlocked or oddly shaped remainder parcels worth significantly less than before.

Agricultural Income Loss

Farms and ranches along the corridor with active agricultural income may have that income discounted or ignored in OTA's initial offer.

Access to the New Turnpike

Properties losing direct access due to the new alignment or its interchanges may suffer severe access damage that must be independently quantified and compensated.

Improvements & Structures

Residential structures, barns, equipment sheds, fences, irrigation systems, and other improvements in the take area must be accurately replaced or compensated at full replacement cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Toby Keith Expressway a toll road?

Yes. The Toby Keith Expressway is a new turnpike being built by the Oklahoma Turnpike Authority (OTA) as part of the ACCESS Oklahoma program. ROW is still acquired under Oklahoma eminent domain procedures, and you have the same rights to negotiate and contest the offered compensation.

My land is near Newcastle, Moore, or Norman — will OTA account for rising land values?

Not necessarily. OTA's appraisers value your land as of the appraisal date using comparable sales. In fast-growing parts of the OKC metro, their data may lag. An independent appraisal can capture current market realities and development potential.

What if my property has future development potential?

Development potential is a legitimate and compensable element of land value. OTA's appraisal may use raw land comparables that don't reflect your property's highest and best use. An independent appraisal can make this case.

Is National ROW a law firm?

No. National ROW is a right-of-way consulting firm, not a law firm. We do not provide legal advice. We help landowners understand their options, quantify damages, and negotiate with OTA.

Which parts of the Toby Keith Expressway are active?

The first segment between I-44 and I-35 is in active right-of-way acquisition, with roughly 20 properties still open and acquisition expected to run through the end of 2026. Other segments remain in active design. If you own land anywhere along the corridor and have been contacted, call us immediately for a free review.

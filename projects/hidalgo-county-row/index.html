What Their Offer May Miss

The agency's appraisers work for the agency — not for you. Their initial offer routinely omits significant categories of compensation you may be entitled to under Texas law.

Agricultural & Ranch Land Value

South Texas farmland and ranchland has specialized value that generic TxDOT appraisals often undercount. We bring in independent appraisers who know the Valley market.

Remainder Tract Damage

When TxDOT takes only part of your property, the land you keep may lose significant value. You're entitled to compensation for that loss — but TxDOT rarely volunteers it.

Irrigation & Water Infrastructure

Water rights, irrigation canals, pumps, and ditches are critical to South Texas farming operations and must be independently valued in any RGV acquisition.

Access & Frontage Loss

Loss of direct highway access, frontage road access, or field access can devastate the operational value of agricultural land and must be fully compensated.

Severance of Tracts

Multiple RGV projects divide farms and ranches into non-contiguous parcels, potentially destroying operational viability — a major compensable damage.

Improvements & Structures

Fences, gates, outbuildings, irrigation infrastructure, and other improvements within the take area have real dollar value and must be accurately accounted for.

I'm not sure which TxDOT project affects my land — can you help?

Absolutely. Call us or fill out the form and describe what TxDOT told you and where your property is located. We'll identify the project and walk you through your rights at no charge.

Can I get more than TxDOT offered, regardless of which project it is?

In most cases, yes. TxDOT's initial offer is based on their appraiser's opinion. An independent review frequently identifies additional compensable items. Our clients across all Pharr District projects regularly receive more than the initial offer.

Is National ROW a law firm?

No. National ROW is a right-of-way consulting firm, not a law firm. We do not provide legal advice. We help landowners understand their options, quantify damages, and negotiate with TxDOT.

About the SH 99 Grand Parkway Project

State Highway 99, known as the Grand Parkway, is a 180-mile toll road forming an outer loop around the Houston metropolitan area. Remaining segments are actively being constructed and developed, requiring ongoing right-of-way acquisition from private landowners across a large geographic footprint.

What TxDOT's Grand Parkway Offer May Miss

The Houston-area land market is one of the fastest-appreciating in Texas. Grand Parkway acquisitions in suburban and exurban areas are especially vulnerable to undervaluation.

Rapidly Appreciating Land Value

Suburban Houston land has appreciated dramatically in recent years. TxDOT's appraisal date may lag actual market conditions — especially in growth corridors like Fort Bend, Montgomery, and Brazoria counties.

Development Potential Loss

Land in the path of the Grand Parkway often has significant development potential. If a taking eliminates your ability to develop a subdivision, commercial site, or industrial tract, that lost potential is compensable.

Remainder Tract Damage

Odd-shaped or landlocked remainder parcels after a taking may be worth significantly less than before — especially for developable suburban tracts.

Agricultural Income Loss

Ranches, farms, and hay operations in the outer counties have active agricultural income that TxDOT's offer may discount or ignore.

Access to Arterials

Properties losing direct access to the Grand Parkway or adjacent frontage roads may suffer severe access damage that must be independently quantified.

Improvements & Structures

Residential structures, barns, equipment sheds, fences, irrigation systems, and other improvements in the take area must be accurately replaced or compensated.

Frequently Asked Questions — Grand Parkway

Is the Grand Parkway operated by TxDOT or a private company?

SH 99 is a toll road managed by the Grand Parkway Transportation Corporation (GPTC) under TxDOT authorization. ROW is still acquired under Texas eminent domain procedures, and you have the same rights to negotiate and contest the offered compensation.

My land is in a rapidly developing area — will TxDOT account for that?

Not necessarily. TxDOT's appraisers value your land as of the appraisal date using comparable sales. In fast-moving suburban Houston markets, their data may lag. An independent appraisal can capture current market realities and development potential.

Is National ROW a law firm?

No. National ROW is a right-of-way consulting firm, not a law firm. We do not provide legal advice. We help landowners understand their options, quantify damages, and negotiate with TxDOT and GPTC.

Which Grand Parkway segments are still active?

Several segments of the outer loop remain under active development with ongoing ROW acquisition. If you own land in any of the seven counties listed and have been contacted, call us immediately for a free review.

Wyoming Eminent Domain Law: W.S. Title 1, Chapter 26

Eminent domain in Wyoming is governed by Wyoming Statutes (W.S.) Title 1, Chapter 26 (Sections 1-26-101 et seq.). Before filing suit, the condemning agency must attempt in good faith to negotiate with the property owner and make a written offer based on a certified appraisal.

Wyoming provides for a jury trial on the issue of just compensation. The owner may also challenge the right to condemn in a separate preliminary hearing. W.S. 1-26-516 permits recovery of attorney fees and costs when the jury award substantially exceeds the agency's last offer.

WYDOT and Wyoming Highway Condemnation

The Wyoming Department of Transportation (WYDOT) acquires highway rights-of-way for state and federal projects. Wyoming has extensive ranch, agricultural, and mineral lands that are frequently crossed by highway corridors. WYDOT must follow W.S. Chapter 26 procedures and federal Uniform Act requirements.

Oil, Gas, and Pipeline Easements in Wyoming

Wyoming is one of the nation's leading oil and natural gas producing states with extensive pipeline infrastructure. Pipeline companies exercising eminent domain must comply with W.S. Chapter 26. Wyoming landowners have the right to compensation for the easement footprint plus any damage to the surrounding property and agricultural operations.

Attorney Fees Under W.S. 1-26-516

Under W.S. 1-26-516, if the jury award exceeds the condemning authority's last offer by a sufficient margin, the court shall award the property owner reasonable attorney fees and litigation expenses. This provision gives Wyoming landowners real leverage when negotiating with agencies that make lowball offers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What law governs eminent domain in Wyoming?

W.S. Title 1, Chapter 26 (Sections 1-26-101 et seq.) governs eminent domain in Wyoming. Agencies must make a written offer based on a certified appraisal before filing suit.

Can I get a jury trial in Wyoming condemnation?

Yes. Wyoming provides for a jury trial on just compensation. Owners may also separately challenge the right to condemn in a preliminary hearing.

Can I recover attorney fees in Wyoming?

Yes. Under W.S. 1-26-516, if the jury award substantially exceeds the agency's last offer, the court awards attorney fees and litigation costs to the property owner.

What is just compensation in Wyoming?

Just compensation is the fair market value of the property or rights taken plus severance damages to the remainder. Wyoming juries determine the final compensation amount.

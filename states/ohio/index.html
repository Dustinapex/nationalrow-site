Ohio Eminent Domain Law: ORC Chapter 163

Eminent domain in Ohio is governed by Ohio Revised Code (ORC) Chapter 163. Before filing a condemnation action, an agency must pass a resolution declaring the necessity of the taking, obtain an independent appraisal, and make a written offer at least 30 days before filing suit. Ohio provides a right to jury trial on just compensation.

ODOT Condemnation and Quick Take

The Ohio Department of Transportation (ODOT) acquires highway rights-of-way under ORC Chapter 5501 and must follow ORC Chapter 163 procedures. Under ORC Section 163.06, ODOT may apply for immediate possession by depositing its appraised value with the court. Owners may withdraw the deposit while litigating the final amount by jury.

Pipeline Easements in Ohio

Ohio is a major pipeline corridor state. Pipeline companies exercising eminent domain must comply with ORC Chapter 163 and applicable federal law. Landowners are entitled to just compensation for the rights granted plus severance damages to the remainder of the property.

Just Compensation and Jury Trial Rights

Just compensation in Ohio is the fair market value of the property or rights taken plus any reduction in value to the remainder. Either party may appeal a commissioners award to a jury. Ohio owners should obtain independent appraisals to document the full value of what is being taken.

Frequently Asked Questions

What law governs eminent domain in Ohio?

ORC Chapter 163 governs Ohio eminent domain. Agencies must pass a resolution of necessity, obtain an appraisal, and make a written offer at least 30 days before filing suit.

div>

Does Ohio have quick-take condemnation?

Yes. Under ORC Section 163.06, agencies may seek immediate possession by depositing the appraised value with the court. Owners may withdraw the deposit while the final amount is determined by jury.

div>

Can I get a jury trial in Ohio condemnation?

Yes. Either party may appeal a commissioners award to a jury. Ohio jury trial rights are among the strongest protections for property owners in the eminent domain process.

div>

What is just compensation in Ohio?

Just compensation is fair market value of the property taken plus severance damages to the remainder. Independent appraisals are essential to document the full value of the taking.

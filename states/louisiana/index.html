Louisiana Eminent Domain Law: La. R.S. Title 19

Eminent domain in Louisiana is governed by Louisiana Revised Statutes Title 19 (Expropriation). Louisiana uses the term "expropriation" rather than "eminent domain" and its civil law tradition gives the process some unique characteristics. Agencies must file an expropriation suit in district court and deposit estimated just compensation before gaining possession.

Louisiana provides for a jury trial on just compensation in expropriation proceedings. The Louisiana Constitution, Article I, Section 4, expressly guarantees that private property shall not be taken except for public purposes and with adequate compensation paid to the owner.

Louisiana DOTD Highway Expropriation

The Louisiana Department of Transportation and Development (DOTD) acquires highway rights-of-way under La. R.S. Title 48 and the expropriation procedures in Title 19. Louisiana has extensive highway, bridge, and levee infrastructure. DOTD must make a bona fide offer prior to filing suit and deposit the offer amount with the court to gain immediate possession.

Pipeline and Utility Servitudes in Louisiana

Louisiana uses the civil law concept of "servitude" for pipeline and utility easements. Pipeline companies with expropriation authority must comply with La. R.S. Title 19. Louisiana landowners have the right to compensation for the servitude footprint plus severance damages to the remainder of the property and any crop, timber, or other agricultural damages.

Just Compensation in Louisiana Expropriation

Just compensation in Louisiana is the fair market value of the property taken plus severance damages to the remainder. Louisiana courts have recognized that full just compensation must include all elements of value, including business damages in appropriate cases. Owners should obtain independent appraisals from Louisiana-licensed appraisers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is expropriation in Louisiana?

Louisiana uses the term "expropriation" for eminent domain, governed by La. R.S. Title 19. Agencies must file an expropriation suit, deposit estimated compensation, and prove public purpose and necessity.

Can Louisiana DOTD take my property immediately?

Yes. Upon depositing estimated just compensation with the court, DOTD may gain immediate possession of the property while compensation is litigated. Owners may withdraw the deposit without prejudicing their right to contest the amount.

Can I get a jury trial in Louisiana expropriation?

Yes. Louisiana provides for a jury trial on just compensation in expropriation proceedings. The Louisiana Constitution guarantees adequate compensation for any taking of private property.

What is just compensation in Louisiana?

Just compensation is the fair market value of the property taken plus severance damages to the remainder. Louisiana courts recognize all elements of value including business damages in appropriate cases.

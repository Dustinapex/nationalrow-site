Pennsylvania Eminent Domain Code: 26 Pa.C.S.

Eminent domain in Pennsylvania is governed by the Pennsylvania Eminent Domain Code, Title 26 of the Pennsylvania Consolidated Statutes (26 Pa.C.S.). Agencies must file a declaration of taking in the Court of Common Pleas, serve it on the owner, and deposit estimated just compensation. The owner then has 30 days to file preliminary objections challenging the right to take or the procedures used.

Pennsylvania's code provides for a board of viewers, a three-person panel appointed by the court, to assess just compensation when the parties cannot agree. Either party may appeal the board's award to a jury trial in the Court of Common Pleas.

PennDOT Condemnation and Right-of-Way

The Pennsylvania Department of Transportation (PennDOT) acquires highway rights-of-way under 26 Pa.C.S. and the federal Uniform Act. PennDOT must file a declaration of taking and deposit estimated just compensation. Owners have the right to challenge the taking's necessity and to contest the compensation amount before a board of viewers or jury.

Pipeline and Utility Easements in Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania has extensive pipeline infrastructure including natural gas, oil, and water transmission lines. Pipeline companies with the right of eminent domain must follow 26 Pa.C.S. procedures. Landowners are entitled to just compensation for the easement rights granted, including damages to the remainder of the property.

Just Compensation Under Pennsylvania Law

Just compensation in Pennsylvania is the fair market value of the property or rights taken, plus severance damages to the remainder. Pennsylvania also allows recovery of delay compensation if the agency fails to pay within the required time, and permits recovery of certain litigation costs in appropriate cases.

Frequently Asked Questions

What law governs eminent domain in Pennsylvania?

The Pennsylvania Eminent Domain Code (26 Pa.C.S.) governs condemnation in Pennsylvania. Agencies must file a declaration of taking and deposit estimated just compensation with the court.

Can I challenge PennDOT's right to take my property?

Yes. Under 26 Pa.C.S. Section 306, property owners have 30 days after service of the declaration of taking to file preliminary objections challenging the right to take, procedural defects, or the adequacy of the bond.

What is a board of viewers in Pennsylvania?

A board of viewers is a three-person panel appointed by the Court of Common Pleas to assess just compensation when the parties cannot agree. Either party may appeal the board's award to a jury trial.

What is just compensation in Pennsylvania?

Just compensation is the fair market value of the property taken plus severance damages to the remainder. Pennsylvania also allows delay compensation if payment is not timely made.

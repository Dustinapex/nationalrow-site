Tennessee Eminent Domain Law: T.C.A. Title 29, Chapter 16

Eminent domain in Tennessee is governed by Tennessee Code Annotated (T.C.A.) Title 29, Chapter 16 (Sections 29-16-101 et seq.). Before filing suit, condemning agencies must make a written offer based on an independent appraisal and attempt to negotiate in good faith with the property owner.

Tennessee provides for a jury trial on just compensation. The condemning authority must deposit its estimate of just compensation with the court before gaining possession. Owners may challenge the right to condemn by filing exceptions within 30 days of service of the condemnation order.

TDOT Highway Condemnation in Tennessee

The Tennessee Department of Transportation (TDOT) acquires highway rights-of-way under T.C.A. Title 54 and the condemnation procedures in T.C.A. Title 29, Chapter 16. Tennessee has extensive interstate and state highway infrastructure. TDOT must follow the federal Uniform Act as well as state procedural requirements.

Pipeline and Utility Easements in Tennessee

Tennessee is crossed by major natural gas, petroleum, and water pipelines. Pipeline companies exercising eminent domain must comply with T.C.A. Title 29, Chapter 16 and applicable federal law. Tennessee landowners have the right to just compensation for the easement granted plus severance damages to the remainder of the property.

Just Compensation in Tennessee

Just compensation in Tennessee is the fair market value of the property or rights taken plus any reduction in value to the remainder caused by the taking. Tennessee courts have recognized that just compensation must be measured from the landowner's perspective, not the condemning agency's. Independent appraisals are essential to document the full impact of the taking.

Frequently Asked Questions

What law governs eminent domain in Tennessee?

T.C.A. Title 29, Chapter 16 governs eminent domain in Tennessee. Agencies must make a written offer based on an independent appraisal and attempt good faith negotiations before filing suit.

Can I challenge the right to condemn my property in Tennessee?

Yes. Tennessee owners may file exceptions within 30 days of service of the condemnation order challenging the right to take or the procedures used by the agency.

Can I get a jury trial in Tennessee condemnation?

Yes. Tennessee provides for a jury trial on just compensation. The owner may demand a jury to determine the fair market value of the property taken and any severance damages.

What is just compensation in Tennessee?

Just compensation is the fair market value of the property or rights taken plus severance damages to the remainder. Tennessee courts measure just compensation from the landowner's perspective.

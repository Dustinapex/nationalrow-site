Virginia Eminent Domain Law Overview

Virginia eminent domain law is governed by Virginia Code Title 25.1. The Virginia Property Rights Act and subsequent amendments require good-faith negotiation, mandatory disclosure of the agency's appraisal, and the right to a commission proceeding before jury trial. Virginia Code §25.1-417 allows courts to award attorney fees when the jury verdict substantially exceeds the condemning authority's final offer.

★ Virginia Code §25.1-417: Attorney Fee Recovery

Virginia law allows courts to award attorney fees to property owners when the jury verdict substantially exceeds the condemning authority's final offer. This provision shifts leverage toward landowners and incentivizes agencies to make fair initial offers.

Frequently Asked Questions — Virginia Eminent Domain

What law governs eminent domain in Virginia?

Virginia Code Title 25.1 governs eminent domain proceedings. The law requires good-faith negotiation, appraisal disclosure, and just compensation. Property owners are entitled to a commission proceeding before the case proceeds to a full jury trial on compensation.

Can I recover attorney fees in Virginia condemnation?

Virginia Code §25.1-417 allows courts to award reasonable attorney fees to property owners when the jury's verdict substantially exceeds the condemning authority's final written offer.

What is just compensation in Virginia?

Virginia requires payment of fair market value for property taken, plus damages to the remainder. The “before and after” method compares your property's value before the taking to its value after — and the difference must be paid.

Do pipeline companies have eminent domain authority in Virginia?

Yes, pipeline companies that qualify as public service companies under Virginia law can exercise eminent domain authority. FERC-regulated interstate pipelines also have federal eminent domain authority. Consult a professional before signing or refusing any easement offer.

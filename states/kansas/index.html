Kansas Eminent Domain Law: K.S.A. Chapter 26

Eminent domain in Kansas is governed primarily by K.S.A. Chapter 26, the Kansas Eminent Domain Procedure Act. Before acquiring property, condemning agencies must make a good faith offer based on an appraisal. If the offer is rejected, the agency may file a condemnation petition in district court.

Kansas provides for appraisers appointed by the court to assess just compensation. Either party may appeal the appraisers award to a jury trial in district court. K.S.A. 26-508 allows recovery of attorney fees and costs when the court award substantially exceeds the agency offer.

KDOT Condemnation in Kansas

The Kansas Department of Transportation (KDOT) acquires highway rights-of-way under K.S.A. Chapter 26 and the federal Uniform Act. Kansas has extensive highway and interstate infrastructure, and KDOT routinely acquires strips of farmland and urban property for road projects. Landowners should not rely solely on KDOT appraisals.

Pipeline and Energy Easements in Kansas

Kansas is a major oil, natural gas, and agricultural state with extensive pipeline infrastructure. Pipeline companies with eminent domain authority must follow K.S.A. Chapter 26 procedures. Wind and solar energy developers also acquire easements across Kansas agricultural land, and landowners should understand their rights before signing.

Attorney Fees Under K.S.A. 26-508

Under K.S.A. 26-508, if the final award exceeds the condemning authority's last written offer by a substantial amount, the court may award the property owner attorney fees and litigation costs. This provision is an important incentive for agencies to make fair initial offers and gives landowners meaningful leverage.

Frequently Asked Questions

What law governs eminent domain in Kansas?

K.S.A. Chapter 26 (the Kansas Eminent Domain Procedure Act) governs condemnation in Kansas. Agencies must make a good faith offer and, if rejected, may file a condemnation petition in district court.

Can I get a jury trial in Kansas condemnation?

Yes. Either party may appeal the court-appointed appraisers award to a jury trial in district court. Kansas jury trials on condemnation are a strong protection for property owners.

Can I recover attorney fees in Kansas condemnation?

Yes. Under K.S.A. 26-508, if the final award substantially exceeds the agency's last written offer, the court may award attorney fees and costs to the property owner.

What is just compensation in Kansas?

Just compensation in Kansas is the fair market value of the property or rights taken, plus any reduction in value to the remainder of the property caused by the taking.

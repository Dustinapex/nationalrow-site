Guide — Partial Takings

What Are “Damages to the Remainder”?

When only part of your property is taken, you may also be owed compensation for the lost value of the land you keep — often the largest and most overlooked part of a claim.

National ROW › Guides › Damages to the remainder

What causes remainder damage

When an agency takes a strip, it can change the shape, access, and usability of everything left behind. Common causes: lost or rerouted access, an odd leftover shape, a strip that splits the parcel, reduced usable acreage, and proximity to a new highway, tower, or pipeline.

How it is measured

Appraisers use a before-and-after approach: the value of the whole property before the taking compared to the remainder after. The difference minus the value of the strip itself is the remainder damage.

Why it gets undercounted

An agency offer may value only the strip and skip what happens to the rest. This is especially common in utility and transmission line easements, where a strip across a farm or commercial tract can affect irrigation, drainage, setbacks, or the ability to subdivide.

  • Lost or restricted access to part of the parcel
  • Irregular shape making portions harder to use
  • Proximity to infrastructure reducing market value
  • Reduced ability to develop, farm, or subdivide

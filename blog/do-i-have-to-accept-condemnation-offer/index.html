Do I Have to Accept the Condemnation Offer?

No. The agency offer is an opening position, not a take-it-or-leave-it number — you generally have the right to question it and pursue more.

Why the first offer is rarely the real number

The condemning agency, utility, or pipeline builds its offer from its own appraisal. That number is designed to get the project moving — and it commonly leaves money on the table, especially for the damage done to the land you keep.

What you can do instead of signing

Get an independent read on value before you respond. Make sure the claim reflects the whole picture: the land or easement taken, the damage to the remainder, lost or changed access, and any impact to buildings, crops, or operations.

  • Review the appraisal assumptions
  • Claim damages to the remainder, not just the strip taken
  • Document lost access, severance, and operational impacts

What it does not mean

Questioning the value usually will not stop the project. But it can change what you are paid — sometimes substantially. Under the Fifth Amendment just compensation requirement, you are entitled to fair market value for what is taken plus any reduction in value to what remains.
